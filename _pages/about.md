I am a Ph.D. candidate in Electrical Engineering at the [Federal University of Pernambuco (UFPE)](https://www.ufpe.br/), Brazil, advised by [Prof. Juliano B. Lima](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=example) (UFPE) and [Prof. Fernando A. N. Santos](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=example) (University of Amsterdam). My doctoral thesis is titled *"Higher-Order Signal Processing and Its Applications in Brain Network Analysis"*.

My research sits at the intersection of **signal processing**, **mathematics**, and **neuroscience**. Specifically, I work on:

- **Topological Signal Processing** — extending classical signal processing to cell complexes and multicomplexes via Hodge and cross-Laplacian operators
- **Graph and Hypergraph Signal Processing** — developing theory and algorithms for signals on higher-order relational structures
- **Brain Network Analysis** — applying topological and higher-order methods to model functional connectivity from EEG/fMRI data, with applications in schizophrenia and ADHD detection

I have been a visiting researcher at the [Korteweg-de Vries Institute for Mathematics (KdVI)](https://kdvi.uva.nl/) and the [Institute for Advanced Study (IAS)](https://ias.uva.nl/) at the University of Amsterdam (Nov. 2023 – Jul. 2024, funded by CAPES), and at [Universitas Mercatorum](https://www.unimercatorum.it/) in Rome (Jul.–Oct. 2025), as part of the TSP-ARK project on topological signal processing for brain and knowledge networks.

Before my Ph.D., I received my M.Sc. (2021) and B.Sc. in Electronic Engineering (2019) from UFPE, where I worked on NFC-based vital sign monitoring and RFID/NFC access control systems, respectively. I also spent a year as an exchange student at [Hochschule Offenburg](https://www.hs-offenburg.de/), Germany (2015–2016), supported by the CAPES Science Without Borders program.
