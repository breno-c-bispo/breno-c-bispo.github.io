I received my Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering (2026) from the [Federal University of Pernambuco (UFPE)](https://www.ufpe.br/), Brazil, advised by [Prof. Juliano B. Lima](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=qIgb2EUAAAAJ&hl=en) (UFPE) and [Prof. Fernando A. N. Santos](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=UUCiHTAAAAAJ&hl=en) (University of Amsterdam). My doctoral thesis is titled *"Higher-Order Signal Processing and Its Applications in Brain Network Analysis"*.

My research lies at the intersection of **signal processing**, **algebraic topology**, **network science**, and **computational neuroscience**. Currently, I'm developing computational frameworks for characterizing complex systems governed by pairwise and higher-order relational structures — including, but not limited to, the human brain. 
Concretely, I work on:

- **Higher-Order Signal Processing** — developing signal processing frameworks over graphs, hypergraphs, simplicial complexes, cell complexes, and multicomplexes; using spectral, algebraic, and topological tools to model pairwise and multiway interactions in complex systems
- **Topology Inference and Network Modeling** — designing data-driven methods to infer relational and higher-order structures from observed signals, with emphasis on sparse, interpretable, and physically meaningful representations of complex dynamics
- **Computational Neuroscience & Brain Network Analysis** — applying higher-order and topological methods to multimodal neuroimaging data, including fMRI, dMRI, and EEG, to characterize functional connectivity, structure–function coupling, brain dynamics, and multi-node interactions; with translational applications in cognitive profiling and neurological or psychiatric disorders
- **Machine Learning for Complex Systems** — developing interpretable learning and classification pipelines based on graph, hypergraph, and topological features, with applications to brain network phenotyping, clinical classification, and behavior-related prediction

I have been a visiting researcher at the [Korteweg-de Vries Institute for Mathematics (KdVI)](https://kdvi.uva.nl/) and the [Institute for Advanced Study (IAS)](https://ias.uva.nl/) at the University of Amsterdam (Nov. 2023 – Jul. 2024, CAPES-funded), and at [Universitas Mercatorum](https://www.unimercatorum.it/) in Rome and the University of Amsterdam (Jul.–Oct. 2025), as part of the [TSP-ARK project](https://www.unimercatorum.it/eventi/cutting-edge-perspectives-on-brain-networks) — *Topological Signal Processing: Applications in bRain and Knowledge Networks* — supervised by [Prof. Stefania Sardellitti](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=VooFNuQAAAAJ&hl=en) and Prof. Fernando A. N. Santos.

Before my Ph.D., I received my M.Sc. in Electrical Engineering (2021, CNPq-funded) from UFPE, where I developed batteryless NFC-based wearable devices for vital sign monitoring. I also hold a B.Sc. in Electronic Engineering (2019) from UFPE, with a thesis on RFID/NFC-based access control systems. During my undergraduate studies, I spent a year as an exchange student at [Hochschule Offenburg](https://www.hs-offenburg.de/), Germany (2015–2016), supported by the CAPES Science Without Borders program, where I studied Electrical Engineering and Information Technology.
